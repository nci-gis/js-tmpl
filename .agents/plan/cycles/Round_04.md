## Why this round exists (not Round 01)

Two reasons, per the PDCA governance rules in
[.agents/plan/PDCA.md](../PDCA.md):

1. **Rounds are append-only.** Round 01 must remain in its original
   `Planning` state as a historical artifact. Quietly retitling or
   re-dating it would rewrite history and hide the out-of-order execution.
2. **Round 02 and Round 03 have landed in git, so the numeric order is
   already fixed.** The honest move is a new round that acknowledges the
   sequence and points back at Round 01 for the (still valid) plan.

## What changed between Round 01's drafting (2026-04-05) and now

Round 02 and Round 03 shipped first, so a handful of assumptions in the
Round 01 plan are worth re-validating before copying its checklist
verbatim:

- **Strict templates (VP-9) landed.** `contentRenderer.js` now compiles
  with `strict: true`. Any helper registered via `registerHelpers` must
  behave correctly under strict mode — verify by spot-checking that
  `{{helperName missing}}` against a missing var still surfaces the
  strict-mode error rather than being swallowed by the helper.
- **Handlebars instance is passed through `renderDirectory(cfg, hbs)`.**
  Round 01's design already depends on this; Round 03 didn't change the
  contract. Still correct.
- **`src/utils/namespacing.js` exists.** If helper name validation is
  added, decide whether to reuse `SEGMENT_RE` or keep the
  bare-identifier rule `/^[a-zA-Z_$][\w$-]*$/` Round 01 proposed.
  Recommendation: keep Round 01's rule (helper names allow hyphens —
  `date-format` is idiomatic; namespacing rule disallows them).
- **`doc-update` skill is stricter about internal vs user-facing docs.**
  When Round 04 Phase "docs" runs, apply the classification policy —
  Round 01 predates this skill.
- **`classifySegment` / path formula language is in place.** Helpers are
  invoked via `{{helperName}}` (template-content only), not in paths, so
  no interaction with Path Guards. Confirm none of the helper-name tests
  accidentally collide with formula syntax regexes.
